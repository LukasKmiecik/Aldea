Manifest

Why does Aldea exist?

A dignified life over constant rush

In Aldea, the goal is not maximizing GDP but quality of life: time for loved ones, healthy food, room for art, rest, growth, experimentation, and the right to fail.

Self-sufficiency over dependency

The settlement aims for high independence in energy, food, and infrastructure ‚Äî not to isolate, but to cooperate with the world on equal terms.

Technology in service of people

Technology is not meant to replace relationships ‚Äî it should reduce repetitive, heavy, and boring work so more energy remains for what is truly human.

Areas of technology and practice

Energy

Microgrids, storage, load management, and consumption prioritization ‚Äî powered by solar, wind, and other locally available sources.

  • small scale, many sources
  • efficiency over raw power
  • designed for on-site maintenance

Water and resource loops

Rain capture, purification, greywater reuse, and organic matter cycling. Closed loops where possible.

Food

Forest gardens, greenhouses, aquaponics, and cooperation with local farms ‚Äî to reduce dependence on external supply chains.

Buildings and infrastructure

Simple, repairable structures, modular buildings, local materials, flexible spaces (work / living / learning / culture).

Communication and organization

Transparent decision tools, open access to information, processes built on trust rather than micromanagement.

Education and culture

Aldea Academy ‚Äî knowledge exchange across generations, artists, engineers, and makers. Education as daily practice, not only a formal system.
